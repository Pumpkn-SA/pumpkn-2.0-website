Business Funding FAQ
Answers for South African SMEs
‍

Who qualifies for a Pumpkn loan?

To qualify, your business must be CIPC-registered, have a business bank account, and have traded for at least 1 year (food businesses) or 3 years (agri businesses). You’ll also need a minimum annual turnover of R1m.

What documents do I need to apply?

We’ll need a copy of the business owner’s ID, your CIPC registration document, and 6 months of recent business bank statements. You’ll also complete our quick onboarding form to get started.

What can Pumpkn loans be used for?

Our loans are designed to help established agri and food businesses manage cash flow and support growth. They can be used for working capital, stock purchases, or day-to-day expenses.

Do you fund startups, grants, or long-term purchases?

Unfortunately not at this stage - we don’t fund startups, offer grants, or finance long-term purchases like land or motor vehicles. Our loans are strictly commercial and carry standard fees.

How quickly can I access funding once I apply?

Once we’ve received your documents and completed our review, approved businesses can typically access funding within a few business days.

Do I need to own land or property to qualify?

Not necessarily. Many of our loans are unsecured, meaning you don't need to put up property as collateral.

What are the minimum requirements to apply for finance?

You'll need:
- A registered business.
- At least R500,000 in annual revenue.
- Six months of bank statements.
Additional requirements may apply depending on your chosen loan and your business's risk profile.

How do I apply?

Explore our financing options on this page. Click "Apply Now" or "Get Approved For Free." Follow the simple online instructions – it takes less than 10 minutes!

How do I access funds if I'm approved?

Once approved, simply submit your supplier invoices, and we'll pay them directly within 24 hours. We generally don't deposit funds directly into your bank account.
